ROME

t’s easy to see why Rome’s one of the most-visited places on the planet: There’s history everywhere (the Pantheon, the Colosseum, the list goes on), sculptural masterpieces in almost every piazza, and—of course—ridiculously good food. Every trip could feel like a whirlwind, but slow down and you’ll discover lots of surprises. Spend a Sunday morning in Trastevere and hunt for vintage finds at Porta Portese flea market. Or hit San Lorenzo—a student neighbourhood with an edgy-but-charming vibe—for trendy shops, galleries, and street art.

Days Required(Avg): 3-5 Days

THINGS TO DO

    1. Historical Sites

  • Colosseum and Roman Forum: Explore ancient Rome's grandeur, including the iconic Colosseum and the Forum, which served as the city's center.
    Pantheon: A marvel of ancient architecture with its famous oculus and stunning interiors.
    Vatican City: Visit St. Peter’s Basilica and its dome for breathtaking views. Tour the Vatican Museums, including the Sistine Chapel with Michelangelo's frescoes. Palatine Hill
    Walk through the origins of Rome with panoramic views of the Forum.

    • 2. Art and Culture:

  • Piazza Navona : Admire Bernini's Fountain of the Four Rivers and enjoy the lively atmosphere.
    Trevi Fountain : Toss a coin to ensure your return to Rome.
    Capitoline Museums: Discover an impressive collection of classical art and artifacts.

    3. Relaxation and Local Life:

  • Trastevere Neighborhood : Stroll through charming cobblestone streets, dine at authentic trattorias, and soak in the local vibe.
    Villa Borghese Gardens : A serene escape with picturesque landscapes and the Borghese Gallery for art enthusiasts.

    • 4. Shopping and Dining

  • Campo de' Fiori and Testaccio Markets : Enjoy fresh produce, local foods, and vibrant market life.
    Via del Corso and Via Condotti : Great for shopping ranging from high-end boutiques to local brands.

    5. Unique experiences

  • Catacombs and Underground Rome : Discover the city's ancient burial grounds and hidden layers beneath the surface.
    Appian Way (Via Appia Antica): Walk or bike along one of the oldest roads in Rome.
    Food Tours : Taste authentic Roman dishes like Carbonara, Cacio e Pepe, and classic Gelato.

    • 6. Day Trips

  • Tivoli for Villa d’Este and Villa Adriana.
    Ostia Antica, an ancient Roman harbor city.
