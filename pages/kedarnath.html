KEDARNATH

Kedarnath is a sacred town nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India, renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty and spiritual significance. At an elevation of 3,583 meters, it is home to the revered Kedarnath Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva and a key site in the Chota Char Dham pilgrimage. Surrounded by snow-capped peaks and serene landscapes, Kedarnath offers a profound spiritual experience and awe-inspiring vistas.

Days Required(Avg): 3-5 Days

THINGS TO DO

    Activities in Kedarnath:

  • Kedarnath Temple: Attend the morning and evening aarti at this sacred temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.
    Bhairav Temple: Situated near the main temple, it offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
    Gandhi Sarovar: A serene lake located about 3 km from Kedarnath, ideal for short treks.
    Vasuki Tal: An 8 km trek from Kedarnath leads to this high-altitude lake, surrounded by majestic peaks.
    Shankaracharya Samadhi: Visit the memorial of Adi Shankaracharya, located behind the temple.
    .

    BEST TIME TO VISIT

  • The optimal period to visit Kedarnath is from May to June and September to October, when the weather is pleasant and the temple is open to pilgrims. JEEVAN GUPTA .

    SOME TRAVEL TIPS

  • Accommodation: Book in advance, especially during peak seasons, to ensure availability.
    Physical Preparation: The trek to Kedarnath is moderately challenging; engage in preparatory fitness routines to enhance endurance.
    Altitude Sickness: Be aware of the symptoms and take necessary precautions, such as acclimatizing properly and staying hydrated.
    Packing Essentials: Include warm clothing, comfortable trekking shoes, rain gear, a first-aid kit, and necessary medications.
    Permits and Registrations: Complete the required registrations with the Uttarakhand Tourism Department prior to your trip.
    Embarking on a journey to Kedarnath is a spiritually enriching experience, offering a blend of devotion and natural beauty.
