JAIPUR

If you take one look at the glorious stucco buildings that line Jaipur's wide streets, you'll understand why this is nicknamed "The Pink City." Spend your days exploring City Palace, Hawa Mahal, and Amber and Jaigarh forts. And if you're looking for a unique souvenir, head to one of the bazaars, where you can pick up a pair of camel-leather slippers.

Days Required(Avg): 3-5 Days

THINGS TO DO

    1. Historical Monuments

  • Amber Fort: Explore this majestic fort, including the Sheesh Mahal (Hall of Mirrors) and scenic views from the top.
    Jaigarh Fort: Known for its massive cannon, Jaivana, and panoramic views of Jaipur.
    Nahargarh Fort: Visit during sunset for a breathtaking view of the city.

    • 2. City Attractions

  • City Palace: A blend of Rajasthani and Mughal architecture, housing museums and royal artifacts.
    Jantar Mantar: A UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for its astronomical instruments.
    Hawa Mahal: Also known as the Palace of Winds, it’s perfect for photos and learning about Jaipur’s history.

    3. Cultural Experiences

  • Albert Hall Museum: For history and art enthusiasts.
    Chokhi Dhani: A cultural village showcasing Rajasthani culture, food, and entertainment.
    Local Markets: Shop at Johari Bazaar, Bapu Bazaar, and Tripolia Bazaar for jewelry, textiles, and handicrafts.

    • 4. Optional Activities

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: For a bird’s eye view of Jaipur.
    Jal Mahal: Admire this water palace, especially during sunrise or sunset.
    Elephant Rides: Near Amber Fort (ensure ethical treatment of animals).
    .

    TIPS

  • Plan your visit during winter (October–March) for pleasant weather.
    Allocate time for local Rajasthani cuisine like dal baati churma, ghevar, and pyaaz kachori.
