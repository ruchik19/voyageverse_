LAKSHWADEEP

The name of India's tiniest Union Territory means "100,000 islands" in Maylayam, the local language; but in truth, there are 36 isles in this archipelago, 10 that are inhabited. In these palm-laden coral islands, a northern extension of the Maldives, diving reigns supreme. Both Kavaratti and Kadmat have full-service scuba facilities, and the latter also has a center for kayaking, water skiing, yachting and wind surfing. Teardrop-shaped Bangaram woos couples with its uninhabited white sands.

Days Required(Avg): 3-5 Days

THINGS TO DO

    1. Water Sports:

  • Lakshadweep is renowned for its crystal-clear waters, making it perfect for activities like snorkeling, scuba diving, kayaking, and windsurfing. Agatti, Bangaram, and Kadmat are popular spots for these.

    • 2. Beach Relaxation

  • The islands are known for their pristine, white-sand beaches like the ones on Kalapeni, Kadmat, and Agatti islands, ideal for unwinding and enjoying the sun
    • <

    Cultural, Spritual Experiences and Relaxation

    3. Coral Reef Exploration

  • Visit the coral reefs around the islands, which are home to a variety of marine life, perfect for divers and snorkelers.

    • 4. Fishing:

  • Lakshadweep offers opportunities for both traditional and sport fishing. You can try your hand at fishing from the shore or on a boat. .

    • 5. Island Hopping

  • The islands are spread out, and each has its unique charm. You can hop between Agatti, Bangaram, Kadmat, and Minicoy to experience different environments and cultures.

    6. Cultural Exploration

  • Learn about the local culture, traditions, and cuisine. The islands have a unique blend of traditions influenced by the Malabar Coast and Islamic practices

    • 7. Lighthouse Visit

  • .Minicoy Island is famous for its historic lighthouse, offering panoramic views of the surroundings

    • 8. Bird Watching

  • The islands are home to various species of migratory birds, making it an excellent location for birdwatching.
