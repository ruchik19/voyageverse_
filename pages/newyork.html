NEW YORK

The tallest skyscrapers, the biggest museums, the cheesiest pizza. New York City takes everything to the max. It’s easy to see why it’s the most-visited place in the U.S.: Whether you want to check out historic landmarks, catch a Broadway show, or stroll the streets of Brooklyn, there’s no wrong way to do it—and something new to discover every time you go. Spend the day neighbourhood-hopping, from the family-friendly Upper West Side packed with playgrounds and cute restaurants to the gritty-but-cool Lower East Side, each has its own vibe. Browse art galleries in Chelsea, go shopping in SoHo, then grab a drink in the East Village.

Days Required(Avg): 4-5 Days

THINGS TO DO

    Explore

    1. Landmarks and Attractions

  • Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island: Iconic landmarks; book a ferry to visit them. Empire State Building: Stunning city views from the observatory. Times Square: Bright lights, billboards, and a buzzing atmosphere. Central Park: A must-see for its beauty and activities like boating, cycling, and picnicking. Brooklyn Bridge: Walk or bike across for panoramic views of Manhattan and Brooklyn. The Metropolitan Museum of Art (The Met): World-class art and history exhibits

    • 2. Neighbourhoods to EXplore

  • Greenwich Village: Quaint streets, cozy cafes, and bohemian vibes. SoHo: High-end shopping and art galleries. Chinatown: Authentic food and vibrant culture. Harlem: Renowned for its jazz clubs and historic sites.

    Cultural and Unique Experiences

    1. Cultural Experiences

  • Broadway Shows: Watch a play or musical in the Theatre District.
  • Museum of Modern Art (MoMA): Home to works by Van Gogh, Picasso, and Warhol.
  • American Museum of Natural History: Famous for its vast exhibits, including dinosaurs.

    • 2. Unique Experiences

  • The High Line: A scenic elevated park on an old railway line.
  • One World Observatory: Panoramic views from the top of the One World Trade Center.
  • Chelsea Market: A food lover’s paradise with diverse cuisines.
  • Edge at Hudson Yards: A thrilling glass-floor sky deck.

    Shopping, Nightlife , Food and dining

    1. Nightlife Entertainment

  • Rooftop bars with skyline views (e.g., 230 Fifth Rooftop Bar).
  • Live music venues like the Blue Note or Apollo Theater.
  • Comedy clubs such as the Comedy Cellar.

    • 2. Shopping

  • Fifth Avenue for luxury brands.
  • Macy's Herald Square for department store shopping.
  • Flea markets and unique boutiques in Williamsburg, Brooklyn..

    • 3. Food and Dining

  • Try a classic New York-style pizza.
  • Enjoy a bagel with cream cheese or smoked salmon.
  • Visit Katz's Delicatessen for a famous pastrami sandwich.
  • Experience fine dining at Michelin-starred restaurants or street food like hot dogs..
