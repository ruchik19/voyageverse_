GOA

East meets West in this sun-soaked state, where Indian culture intertwines with Portuguese influences left over from a 500-year occupation. The beaches have long served as a magnet for serene hedonists. To the north, the tourist-centric scene is prevalent, with an international flair that is now skewing more hip than hippie. Travel south for stretches of unspoiled sand and an escape from large resorts. Temples, mosques and wildlife sanctuaries provide diversions from the beach.

Days Required(Avg): 4-7 Days

THINGS TO DO

    Beaches, Watersports and Nightlife

    1. Beaches

  • Baga Beach: Nightlife and water sports.
  • Anjuna Beach: Flea markets and scenic beauty.
  • Calangute Beach: Popular for family outings and water activities
  • Palolem Beach: Tranquil and serene.
  • Colva Beach: Perfect for a quiet retreat.
  • Agonda Beach: Great for solitude seekers..

    • 2. Watersports

  • Parasailing
  • Jet Skiing
  • Scuba Diving (near Grande Island)
  • Windsurfing
  • Kayaking in backwaters

    • 3. Nightlife

  • Club Cubana
  • Titos Lane (Baga)
  • Silent Noise Parties (Palolem)

    Historical sites,Nature & Wildlife

    1. Historical Sites

  • Basilica of Bom Jesus (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
  • Se Cathedral
  • Church of St. Francis of Assisi.
  • Aguada Fort
  • Chapora Fort (famous from the movie Dil Chahta Hai)

    • 2. Nature nd Wildlife

  • Dudhsagar Waterfalls
  • Spice Plantations (e.g., Sahakari Spice Farm)
  • Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary

    Markets, Food Experiences

    1. Markets

  • Anjuna Flea Market (Wednesdays)
  • Mapusa Market (Fridays)
  • Saturday Night Market at Arpora

    • 2. Food Experiences

  • Fresh seafood at beach shacks
  • Local Goan dishes like Vindaloo, Xacuti, and Bebinca
  • Feni (a local liquor made from cashew or coconut)
