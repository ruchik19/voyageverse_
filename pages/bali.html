BALI

Bali packs a lot into one small island— from breathtaking waterfalls like Sekumpul in the north to the white sand beaches of Nyang Nyang in the south. Whatever you’re seeking, you’ll probably find: surf-able waves in Batu Bolong, all-night clubs in Seminyak, luxury clifftop hotels in Nusa Dua, and spiritual refuges all around. Snap a pic at the Gateway to Heaven, cut through the jungle to the hidden Gunung Kawi Temple, and catch the sunset and a traditional fire dance at Uluwatu Temple. Just don’t miss the markets—hunt for handcrafted artwork and textiles at the Sukawati Art Market, or snack on street food staples like satay and bakso at the Sindhu Night Market.

Days Required(Avg): 7-10 Days

THINGS TO DO

    Beaches and Water Activities

    1. Beaches and Water Activities

  • Kuta Beach: Surfing and vibrant nightlife.
  • Seminyak and Canggu: Relaxing beaches with luxury resorts and beach clubs.
  • Nusa Dua: Ideal for family-friendly beaches and water sports.
  • Nusa Penida Island: Stunning cliffs, snorkeling, and diving.
  • Blue Lagoon: Snorkeling in crystal-clear waters.
  • Great for photography enthusiasts.

    • 2. Food and Nightlife

  • Local Cuisine: Try Babi Guling (roast pork), Bebek Betutu (slow-cooked duck), and fresh seafood..
  • Nightlife: Beach clubs like Potato Head and Finns, or the vibrant bars in Seminyak and Kuta..

    Cultural Experiences

    1. Ubud

  • Visit the Ubud Monkey Forest.
  • Explore traditional markets and art galleries.
  • Attend a Balinese dance performance.

    • 2. Temples

  • Tanah Lot: A scenic temple on a rocky outcrop.
  • Uluwatu Temple: Perched on a cliff with sunset views.
  • Besakih Temple: Bali's "Mother Temple."

    • 3.Balinese Cooking Classes

  • Learn to prepare traditional dishes like nasi goreng and satay.

    Nature and Adventure

    1.Nature and Adventure

  • Mount Batur: Sunrise trekking for panoramic views.
  • Tegalalang Rice Terraces: Iconic green terraces for scenic walks.
  • Sekumpul and Gitgit Waterfalls: Picturesque spots for nature lovers.
  • Jatiluwih Rice Fields: UNESCO World Heritage Site with vast landscapes.

    • 2.Off-the-Beaten-Path

  • Sidemen Valley: Quiet countryside with stunning landscapes.
  • Amed and Tulamben: Ideal for diving and exploring shipwrecks.
  • Munduk: A mountain village with cool weather and waterfalls.
